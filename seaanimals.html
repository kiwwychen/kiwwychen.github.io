God of Agriculture-Whales

Whales lives in the deep ocean. They returns to
the surface of the ocean to breathe and defecate.
Because its fece is rich in iron minerals,
it creates nice environment for the phytoplankton.

God of Night-Krill

Krill eats phytoplankton for life. Krill is not
good at digesting and absorbing, so their feces is
rich in carbon content. Because the amount
of krill's feces are too many, the phytoplankton
can't consume their feces totally. Krill often stays
in the deep sea at night, so we call it “the God of Night”

God of Move-Zooplankton

The Zooplanktons which live in the middle water
layer often go to the surface at night. They would
eat phytoplanktons or other small zooplanktons
at that time. Then, they go back to the deep
sea to hide from predators. The migration
between the surface and deep sea is said to be the
biggest migration on Earth, so we call it
“the God of Move.”
